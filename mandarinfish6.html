Angel Fish

The Angel Fish (Pterophyllum scalare) is a popular aquarium fish known for its elegant appearance, graceful fins, and peaceful nature. It is native to the Amazon River Basin in South America.

Physical Characteristics:

  • Size: 6 – 8 inches (15 – 20 cm)
  • Lifespan: 10 – 15 years in captivity
  • Colors: Silver, gold, black, and marble patterns
  • Body Shape: Disc-shaped body with long, flowing fins

Habitat & Tank Requirements:

  • Water Type: Freshwater
  • Tank Size: Minimum 20 gallons
  • Water Temperature: 24 – 28°C (75 – 82°F)
  • pH Level: 6.5 – 7.5

Diet & Feeding:

  • Main Diet: Flake food, pellets, live and frozen food (such as brine shrimp)
  • Feeding Frequency: 2 – 3 times per day
  • Supplementary Diet: Vegetables like spinach or zucchini

Behavior & Tank Mates:

  • Personality: Peaceful, but can be territorial when breeding
  • Aggression Level: Low to moderate
  • Compatible Tank Mates: Tetras, Gouramis, and other peaceful species

Breeding:

  • Maturity Age: 6 – 12 months
  • Breeding Conditions: Stable water temperature, flat surfaces for egg laying
  • Egg-Layers: Lays eggs on vertical surfaces like plant leaves or rocks

Common Health Issues:

  • Ich (White Spot Disease): Can be treated with aquarium salt and medication
  • Fin Rot: Caused by poor water conditions, treated with antibiotics
  • Swim Bladder Disease: Due to overfeeding or water quality issues
