Carp Fish

The Carp fish (Cyprinus carpio) is a hardy freshwater fish known for its adaptability and widespread presence in aquaculture. It is commonly found in ponds, lakes, and rivers worldwide.

Physical Characteristics:

  • Size: 12 – 40 inches (30 – 100 cm)
  • Lifespan: Up to 20 – 50 years
  • Colors: Silver, golden, brown, orange (Koi variety)
  • Body Shape: Deep-bodied with large scales

Habitat & Tank Requirements:

  • Tank/Pond Size: Minimum 250 gallons (ponds are preferred)
  • Water Temperature: 10 – 25°C (50 – 77°F)
  • pH Level: 6.5 – 8.5
  • Filtration: Strong filtration for clean water

Diet & Feeding:

  • Main Diet: Algae, plants, insects, crustaceans
  • Pellets & Grains: Wheat, rice bran, and fish pellets
  • Fruits & Vegetables: Peas, lettuce, watermelon
  • Feeding Frequency: 1 – 2 times daily

Behavior & Tank Mates:

  • Personality: Social and peaceful
  • Aggression Level: Non-aggressive, schooling fish
  • Compatible Tank Mates: Koi, Goldfish, Loaches, Catfish

Breeding:

  • Maturity Age: 3 – 4 years
  • Breeding Season: Spring to early summer
  • Egg-Layers: Lays thousands of eggs on aquatic plants

Common Health Issues:

  • Parasitic Infections: Anchor worms, flukes
  • Fungal Diseases: Cotton-like patches on the body
  • Swim Bladder Disease: Causes difficulty in swimming
