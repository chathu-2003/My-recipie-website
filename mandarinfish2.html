Guppy Fish

The Guppy Fish (Poecilia reticulata) is a vibrant, easy-to-care-for freshwater fish known for its beautiful colors and active nature. Originally from South America, guppies are one of the most popular aquarium fish worldwide.

Physical Characteristics:

  • Size: 1.5 – 2.5 inches (4 – 6 cm)
  • Lifespan: 2 – 5 years with proper care
  • Colors: Red, blue, yellow, green, and multi-colored patterns

Habitat & Tank Requirements:

  • Tank Size: Minimum 10 gallons (40 liters) for a small group
  • Water Temperature: 22 – 28°C (72 – 82°F)
  • Filtration: Required to maintain water quality

Diet & Feeding:

  • Main Diet: High-quality fish flakes with protein
  • Live/Frozen Foods: Brine shrimp, daphnia, and bloodworms
  • Vegetables: Blanched spinach, lettuce, and zucchini

Breeding:

  • Gestation Period: 21 – 30 days
  • Number of Fry: 20 – 50 per birth
  • Special Care: Fry should be kept separate to avoid being eaten by adults

Common Health Issues:

  • Fin Rot: Caused by poor water quality
  • Ich (White Spot Disease): Parasite infection causing white spots
  • Swim Bladder Disorder: Affects fish’s ability to swim properly
