Giant Gourami

The Giant Gourami (Osphronemus goramy) is a large, intelligent freshwater fish known for its impressive size and unique personality. It is often kept in large aquariums and recognized for its ability to interact with its owner.

Physical Characteristics:

  • Size: 18 – 28 inches (45 – 70 cm)
  • Lifespan: Up to 20 years
  • Colors: Silver, yellow, white, pink, albino
  • Special Feature: Develops a bulbous forehead with age

Habitat & Tank Requirements:

  • Tank Size: Minimum 200 gallons (750 liters)
  • Water Temperature: 22 – 30°C (72 – 86°F)
  • pH Level: 6.5 – 8.0
  • Filtration: Strong filtration is necessary

Diet & Feeding:

  • Main Diet: Vegetables like spinach, lettuce, zucchini
  • Protein: Insects, shrimp, small fish
  • Pellets & Flakes: High-quality commercial food
  • Fruits: Occasional treats like bananas and apples

Behavior & Tank Mates:

  • Personality: Intelligent and interactive
  • Aggression Level: Generally peaceful but territorial
  • Compatible Tank Mates: Arowanas, large catfish, Oscars

Breeding:

  • Maturity Age: 3 – 4 years
  • Breeding Tank: At least 250 gallons
  • Egg-Layers: They build bubble nests

Common Health Issues:

  • Fin Rot: Caused by poor water quality
  • Ich (White Spot Disease): Stress-related infection
  • Obesity: Due to overfeeding
