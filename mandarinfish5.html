Filter Feeder Fish

Filter feeder fish are species that consume microscopic plankton, algae, and suspended particles from the water. They help maintain the ecological balance of aquatic environments.

Common Types of Filter Feeder Fish:

  • Whale Shark (Rhincodon typus) - The largest filter feeder
  • Manta Ray (Manta birostris) - Uses cephalic lobes to filter plankton
  • Paddlefish (Polyodon spathula) - Freshwater filter feeder
  • Basking Shark (Cetorhinus maximus) - A slow-moving filter feeder

Physical Characteristics:

  • Size: Varies from 12 inches to over 40 feet
  • Body Shape: Streamlined for efficient filter feeding
  • Feeding Mechanism: Uses gill rakers to trap plankton

Habitat & Tank Requirements (For Small Filter Feeders):

  • Water Type: Freshwater and saltwater species
  • Tank Size: Large tanks (100+ gallons for small species)
  • Water Flow: Moderate to high current
  • pH Level: 6.5 â€“ 8.5

Diet & Feeding:

  • Primary Diet: Phytoplankton, zooplankton, microalgae
  • Feeding Method: Passive filter feeding
  • Supplementary Diet: Liquid plankton supplements (for aquarium species)

Behavior & Tank Mates:

  • Personality: Gentle and slow-moving
  • Aggression Level: Completely non-aggressive
  • Compatible Tank Mates: Non-predatory species, shrimp, mollusks

Common Health Issues:

  • Water Quality Sensitivity: Requires clean, well-oxygenated water
  • Gill Disease: Due to clogged gill rakers
  • Starvation: Needs a consistent plankton supply
